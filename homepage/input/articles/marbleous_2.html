The Novice — "Easy?" — Dragging the Boxes

Walking through the Tutorial, our novice is confronted with probable, exemplary "scenarios":
For exmaple with water!
How do I get to the other site &mdsh; and he sees those wooden boxes, "st_wood" — this stone is one of the most fundamental in Enigma: Movable, onto-something-movable, away-from-something-movable, building "bridges" in water and abyss, combinable — thus its immense spread across all of Enigma's levels: In sokoban-like games, puzzle-games, landscapes. And the player asks himself quite often, e.g. in "Waterworld", how many "st_woods" have to be placed correctly to get nearer to the solution: push here, push there, but push correctly!
And then our novice starts the level "Easy?". Like within a nightmare he thinks about these "fascinating" logical exercises of intelligence tests, "which everyone is freightening of — confess it!" — and he thinks by himself: Oh dear — where shall all those boxes go? And he starts to work out some approaches to the solution — yes, it really is "dragging the boxes"! Easy? Made it — yes, this is Enigma!
